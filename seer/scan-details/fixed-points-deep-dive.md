## Executive Summary

Fixed points are events with probability >0.95 that cannot be prevented. They represent the highest-confidence predictions in the Seer methodology. Two fixed points are identified for this decade.

## What Are Fixed Points?

### Definition

Fixed points are events that:
- Have probability >0.95 of occurrence
- Cannot be prevented by current actions
- Can only be prepared for
- Represent deterministic elements in the forecast

### Methodology

Fixed points are identified through:
1. Q3 services integration
2. World feed pattern analysis
3. Cross-methodology convergence
4. Historical probability validation

## The Nature of Fixed Points

### What Makes Them Fixed

1. **Causal chains already in motion** - Too late to redirect
2. **Multiple convergent pathways** - Many roads lead to same event
3. **External determinism** - Factors outside human control
4. **Probabilistic threshold** - Beyond uncertainty range

### What This Means

| Implication | Response |
|-------------|----------|
| Cannot prevent | Accept and prepare |
| High confidence | Plan with certainty |
| Fixed timing | Calibrate preparation timeline |
| Known impact | Design mitigation strategies |
